1.1

Tangent Lines and Rates of Change

Theory and Formulas

Definition: The slope of the tangent line to \(y = f(x)\) at \(x = a\) is:

\[ m = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f(a+h) - f(a)}{h} \]

This represents the instantaneous rate of change of \(f\) at \(x = a\).

Examples (7+)

1
Basic
Find the slope of the tangent line to \(f(x) = x^2\) at \(x = 3\).

\(m = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f(3+h) - f(3)}{h} = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{(3+h)^2 - 9}{h}\)

\(= \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{9 + 6h + h^2 - 9}{h} = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{6h + h^2}{h}\)

\(= \lim_{h \to 0} (6 + h) = 6\)

2
Equation
Find the equation of the tangent line to \(f(x) = \sqrt{x}\) at \(x = 4\).

First find slope: \(m = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{\sqrt{4+h} - 2}{h}\)

Multiply by conjugate: \(m = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{(\sqrt{4+h} - 2)(\sqrt{4+h} + 2)}{h(\sqrt{4+h} + 2)}\)

\(= \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{4+h-4}{h(\sqrt{4+h}+2)} = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{1}{\sqrt{4+h}+2} = \frac{1}{4}\)

Equation: \(y - 2 = \frac{1}{4}(x - 4)\) or \(y = \frac{1}{4}x + 1\)

3
Physics
A ball's height is \(h(t) = -16t^2 + 64t\). Find instantaneous velocity at \(t = 2\).

Velocity = rate of change: \(v(2) = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{h(2+h) - h(2)}{h}\)

\(h(2) = -16(4) + 64(2) = 64\)

\(h(2+h) = -16(4+4h+h^2) + 128 + 64h = 64 - 16h^2\)

\(v(2) = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{64 - 16h^2 - 64}{h} = \lim_{h \to 0} (-16h) = 0\) ft/s

4
Rational
Find slope of tangent to \(f(x) = \frac{1}{x}\) at \(x = 2\).

\(m = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{\frac{1}{2+h} - \frac{1}{2}}{h} = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{2 - (2+h)}{2h(2+h)}\)

\(= \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{-h}{2h(2+h)} = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{-1}{2(2+h)} = -\frac{1}{4}\)

5
Cubic
Find where tangent to \(f(x) = x^3 - 3x\) is horizontal.

Horizontal tangent ⇒ slope = 0

\(m = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{(x+h)^3 - 3(x+h) - (x^3 - 3x)}{h} = 3x^2 - 3\)

Set \(3x^2 - 3 = 0\) ⇒ \(x^2 = 1\) ⇒ \(x = \pm 1\)

6
Average Rate
Find average rate of change of \(f(x) = x^2\) from \(x = 1\) to \(x = 3\).

Average rate = \(\frac{f(3) - f(1)}{3 - 1} = \frac{9 - 1}{2} = 4\)

7
Absolute Value
Does \(f(x) = |x|\) have a tangent line at \(x = 0\)?

Right: \(m_+ = \lim_{h \to 0^+} \frac{|h|}{h} = 1\)

Left: \(m_- = \lim_{h \to 0^-} \frac{|h|}{h} = -1\)

Since \(m_+ \neq m_-\), no tangent line exists at \(x = 0\).

1.2

The Limit

Theory and Formulas

Definition: \(\lim_{x \to a} f(x) = L\) means as \(x\) approaches \(a\), \(f(x)\) approaches \(L\).

\[ \lim_{x \to a} f(x) = L \]

Examples (7+)

1
Direct
Evaluate \(\lim_{x \to 2} (3x - 1)\).

Direct substitution: \(3(2) - 1 = 5\)

2
Standard
Evaluate \(\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sin x}{x}\).

Standard limit: \(\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sin x}{x} = 1\)

3
Polynomial
Evaluate \(\lim_{x \to -1} (x^3 - 2x^2 + 3x - 4)\).

Direct substitution: \(-1 - 2 - 3 - 4 = -10\)

4
Rational
Evaluate \(\lim_{x \to 3} \frac{x^2 - 9}{x - 3}\).

Factor: \(\lim_{x \to 3} \frac{(x-3)(x+3)}{x-3} = \lim_{x \to 3} (x+3) = 6\)

5
Square Root
Evaluate \(\lim_{x \to 4} \frac{\sqrt{x} - 2}{x - 4}\).

Multiply conjugate: \(\lim_{x \to 4} \frac{(\sqrt{x}-2)(\sqrt{x}+2)}{(x-4)(\sqrt{x}+2)} = \lim_{x \to 4} \frac{1}{\sqrt{x}+2} = \frac{1}{4}\)

6
Trigonometric
Evaluate \(\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{1 - \cos x}{x}\).

Multiply by conjugate: \(\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{(1-\cos x)(1+\cos x)}{x(1+\cos x)} = \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sin^2 x}{x(1+\cos x)} = 0\)

7
Exponential
Evaluate \(\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{e^x - 1}{x}\).

Standard limit: \(\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{e^x - 1}{x} = 1\)

Exercises (10+)

\(\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sin x}{x \cos x} = 1\)

6

\(\ln 3 \approx 1.099\)

One-Sided Limits

Theory and Formulas

Definition:

\[ \lim_{x \to a^-} f(x) = L \quad \text{(left-hand limit)} \] \[ \lim_{x \to a^+} f(x) = M \quad \text{(right-hand limit)} \]

\(\lim_{x \to a} f(x)\) exists iff both one-sided limits exist and are equal.

Examples (7+)

1
Absolute Value
Find \(\lim_{x \to 0^+} \frac{|x|}{x}\) and \(\lim_{x \to 0^-} \frac{|x|}{x}\).

Right: \(\lim_{x \to 0^+} \frac{x}{x} = 1\)

Left: \(\lim_{x \to 0^-} \frac{-x}{x} = -1\)

2
Piecewise
Let \(f(x) = \begin{cases} x^2 & x < 1 \\ 2x & x \geq 1 \end{cases}\). Find one-sided limits at \(x=1\).

Left: \(\lim_{x \to 1^-} x^2 = 1\)

Right: \(\lim_{x \to 1^+} 2x = 2\)

3
Step Function
Find \(\lim_{x \to 2^-} \lfloor x \rfloor\) and \(\lim_{x \to 2^+} \lfloor x \rfloor\).

Left: \(\lim_{x \to 2^-} \lfloor x \rfloor = 1\)

Right: \(\lim_{x \to 2^+} \lfloor x \rfloor = 2\)

4
Rational
Find \(\lim_{x \to 0^+} \frac{1}{x}\) and \(\lim_{x \to 0^-} \frac{1}{x}\).

Right: \(\lim_{x \to 0^+} \frac{1}{x} = +\infty\)

Left: \(\lim_{x \to 0^-} \frac{1}{x} = -\infty\)

5
Square Root
Find \(\lim_{x \to 0^+} \sqrt{x}\) and \(\lim_{x \to 0^-} \sqrt{x}\).

Right: \(\lim_{x \to 0^+} \sqrt{x} = 0\)

Left: \(\lim_{x \to 0^-} \sqrt{x}\) does not exist (domain issue)

6
Trigonometric
Find \(\lim_{x \to (\pi/2)^-} \tan x\) and \(\lim_{x \to (\pi/2)^+} \tan x\).

Left: \(\lim_{x \to (\pi/2)^-} \tan x = +\infty\)

Right: \(\lim_{x \to (\pi/2)^+} \tan x = -\infty\)

7
Composite
Find \(\lim_{x \to 1^-} \frac{|x-1|}{x-1}\) and \(\lim_{x \to 1^+} \frac{|x-1|}{x-1}\).

Left: \(\lim_{x \to 1^-} \frac{-(x-1)}{x-1} = -1\)

Right: \(\lim_{x \to 1^+} \frac{x-1}{x-1} = 1\)

Exercises (10+)

1
Absolute Value
Find \(\lim_{x \to 2^+} \frac{|x-2|}{x-2}\).

For \(x>2\), \(|x-2| = x-2\), so limit = \(1\)

2
Piecewise
Find \(\lim_{x \to 0^-} f(x)\) where \(f(x) = \begin{cases} x+1 & x < 0 \\ x^2 & x \geq 0 \end{cases}\).

\(\lim_{x \to 0^-} (x+1) = 1\)

3
Rational
Find \(\lim_{x \to 3^-} \frac{1}{x-3}\).

\(\lim_{x \to 3^-} \frac{1}{x-3} = -\infty\)

4
Square Root
Find \(\lim_{x \to 4^+} \sqrt{x-4}\).

\(\lim_{x \to 4^+} \sqrt{x-4} = 0\)

5
Trigonometric
Find \(\lim_{x \to 0^+} \frac{\sin x}{|x|}\).

\(\lim_{x \to 0^+} \frac{\sin x}{x} = 1\)

6
Exponential
Find \(\lim_{x \to 0^-} e^{1/x}\).

\(\lim_{x \to 0^-} e^{1/x} = 0\)

7
Logarithmic
Find \(\lim_{x \to 0^+} \ln x\).

\(\lim_{x \to 0^+} \ln x = -\infty\)

8
Greatest Integer
Find \(\lim_{x \to 3^-} \lfloor x \rfloor\).

\(\lim_{x \to 3^-} \lfloor x \rfloor = 2\)

9
Composite
Find \(\lim_{x \to 0^+} \frac{\sqrt{x}}{x}\).

\(\lim_{x \to 0^+} \frac{\sqrt{x}}{x} = \lim_{x \to 0^+} \frac{1}{\sqrt{x}} = +\infty\)

10
Two-sided
Determine if \(\lim_{x \to 0} f(x)\) exists for \(f(x) = \begin{cases} x^2 & x \leq 0 \\ x & x > 0 \end{cases}\).

Left: \(\lim_{x \to 0^-} x^2 = 0\), Right: \(\lim_{x \to 0^+} x = 0\), So limit exists and equals \(0\)

1.4

Limit Properties

Theory and Formulas

Limit Properties: Assume \(\lim_{x \to a} f(x) = L\) and \(\lim_{x \to a} g(x) = M\):

1. \(\lim_{x \to a} [f(x) + g(x)] = L + M\)

2. \(\lim_{x \to a} [f(x) - g(x)] = L - M\)

3. \(\lim_{x \to a} [cf(x)] = cL\)

4. \(\lim_{x \to a} [f(x)g(x)] = LM\)

5. \(\lim_{x \to a} \frac{f(x)}{g(x)} = \frac{L}{M}\) (if \(M \neq 0\))

6. \(\lim_{x \to a} [f(x)]^n = L^n\)

7. \(\lim_{x \to a} \sqrt[n]{f(x)} = \sqrt[n]{L}\) (if \(L \geq 0\) for even \(n\))

Examples (7+)

1
Basic
Given \(\lim_{x \to 2} f(x) = 3\) and \(\lim_{x \to 2} g(x) = -1\), find \(\lim_{x \to 2} [2f(x) - 3g(x)^2]\).

\(\lim_{x \to 2} [2f(x) - 3g(x)^2] = 2\lim_{x \to 2} f(x) - 3[\lim_{x \to 2} g(x)]^2\)

\(= 2(3) - 3(-1)^2 = 6 - 3 = 3\)

2
Quotient
Given \(\lim_{x \to 1} f(x) = 4\) and \(\lim_{x \to 1} g(x) = 2\), find \(\lim_{x \to 1} \frac{f(x)}{g(x)-1}\).

\(\lim_{x \to 1} \frac{f(x)}{g(x)-1} = \frac{\lim_{x \to 1} f(x)}{\lim_{x \to 1} g(x) - 1}\)

\(= \frac{4}{2 - 1} = 4\)

3
Product
Given \(\lim_{x \to 0} f(x) = 2\) and \(\lim_{x \to 0} g(x) = 3\), find \(\lim_{x \to 0} f(x)g(x)^2\).

\(\lim_{x \to 0} f(x)g(x)^2 = [\lim_{x \to 0} f(x)][\lim_{x \to 0} g(x)]^2\)

\(= 2 \times 3^2 = 18\)

4
Composite
Given \(\lim_{x \to 3} f(x) = 9\), find \(\lim_{x \to 3} \sqrt{f(x)}\).

\(\lim_{x \to 3} \sqrt{f(x)} = \sqrt{\lim_{x \to 3} f(x)} = \sqrt{9} = 3\)

5
Polynomial
Find \(\lim_{x \to 2} (3x^2 - 2x + 1)\) using limit properties.

\(\lim_{x \to 2} (3x^2 - 2x + 1) = 3\lim_{x \to 2} x^2 - 2\lim_{x \to 2} x + \lim_{x \to 2} 1\)

\(= 3(4) - 2(2) + 1 = 12 - 4 + 1 = 9\)

6
Rational
Find \(\lim_{x \to 1} \frac{x^3 - 1}{x^2 + 2}\) using limit properties.

\(\lim_{x \to 1} \frac{x^3 - 1}{x^2 + 2} = \frac{\lim_{x \to 1} (x^3 - 1)}{\lim_{x \to 1} (x^2 + 2)}\)

\(= \frac{1^3 - 1}{1^2 + 2} = \frac{0}{3} = 0\)

7
Multiple Properties
Given \(\lim_{x \to 4} f(x) = 2\), find \(\lim_{x \to 4} \frac{3f(x)^2 - 2f(x) + 1}{f(x) + 3}\).

\(\lim_{x \to 4} \frac{3f(x)^2 - 2f(x) + 1}{f(x) + 3} = \frac{3(2)^2 - 2(2) + 1}{2 + 3}\)

\(= \frac{12 - 4 + 1}{5} = \frac{9}{5}\)

Computing Limits

Theory and Formulas

Techniques for Computing Limits:

  1. Direct substitution
  2. Factoring and canceling
  3. Rationalizing numerator/denominator
  4. Using standard limits
  5. L'Hôpital's Rule (later)

Examples (7+)

1
Direct Substitution
Find \(\lim_{x \to 2} \frac{x^2 + 3x - 2}{x - 1}\).

Direct substitution: \(\frac{4 + 6 - 2}{2 - 1} = \frac{8}{1} = 8\)

2
Factoring
Find \(\lim_{x \to 2} \frac{x^2 - 4}{x - 2}\).

Factor: \(\frac{(x-2)(x+2)}{x-2} = x+2 = 4\)

3
Rationalizing
Find \(\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sqrt{1+x} - 1}{x}\).

Multiply by conjugate: \(\frac{(\sqrt{1+x}-1)(\sqrt{1+x}+1)}{x(\sqrt{1+x}+1)} = \frac{1+x-1}{x(\sqrt{1+x}+1)}\)

\(= \frac{1}{\sqrt{1+x}+1} = \frac{1}{2}\)

4
Trigonometric
Find \(\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sin 3x}{x}\).

Let \(u = 3x\): \(\lim_{u \to 0} \frac{\sin u}{u/3} = 3\lim_{u \to 0} \frac{\sin u}{u} = 3\)

5
Rational Function
Find \(\lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{3x^2 + 2x - 1}{5x^2 - x + 4}\).

Divide by \(x^2\): \(\lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{3 + \frac{2}{x} - \frac{1}{x^2}}{5 - \frac{1}{x} + \frac{4}{x^2}} = \frac{3}{5}\)

6
Piecewise
Find \(\lim_{x \to 0} f(x)\) where \(f(x) = \begin{cases} x^2 & x < 0 \\ 2x & x \geq 0 \end{cases}\).

Left: \(\lim_{x \to 0^-} x^2 = 0\)

Right: \(\lim_{x \to 0^+} 2x = 0\)

Limit = 0

7
Exponential
Find \(\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{a^x - 1}{x}\) for \(a > 0\).

This is a standard limit: \(\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{a^x - 1}{x} = \ln a\)

Infinite Limits

Theory and Formulas

Definition: \(\lim_{x \to a} f(x) = \infty\) means \(f(x)\) grows without bound as \(x \to a\).

\[ \lim_{x \to a} f(x) = \infty \quad \text{or} \quad \lim_{x \to a} f(x) = -\infty \]

Examples (7+)

1
Basic
Find \(\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{1}{x^2}\).

\(\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{1}{x^2} = \infty\)

2
One-sided
Find \(\lim_{x \to 0^+} \frac{1}{x}\) and \(\lim_{x \to 0^-} \frac{1}{x}\).

1.7

Continuity

Theory and Formulas

Definition: A function \(f\) is continuous at \(x = a\) if:

\[ \lim_{x \to a} f(x) = f(a) \]

This requires: (1) \(f(a)\) exists, (2) \(\lim_{x \to a} f(x)\) exists, (3) They are equal.

Examples (7+)

1
Polynomial
Show that \(f(x) = x^2 + 3x - 2\) is continuous at \(x = 1\).

\(f(1) = 1 + 3 - 2 = 2\)

\(\lim_{x \to 1} (x^2 + 3x - 2) = 1 + 3 - 2 = 2\)

Since \(\lim_{x \to 1} f(x) = f(1)\), \(f\) is continuous at \(x = 1\).

2
Removable Discontinuity
Is \(f(x) = \frac{x^2 - 4}{x - 2}\) continuous at \(x = 2\)?

\(f(2)\) is undefined, so \(f\) is not continuous at \(x = 2\).

However, the discontinuity is removable since \(\lim_{x \to 2} f(x) = 4\) exists.

3
Jump Discontinuity
Check continuity of \(f(x) = \begin{cases} x+1 & x < 0 \\ x^2 & x \geq 0 \end{cases}\) at \(x = 0\).

\(f(0) = 0\)

Left limit: \(\lim_{x \to 0^-} (x+1) = 1\)

Right limit: \(\lim_{x \to 0^+} x^2 = 0\)

Since left limit ≠ right limit, \(\lim_{x \to 0} f(x)\) doesn't exist, so \(f\) is discontinuous at \(x = 0\).

4
Infinite Discontinuity
Check continuity of \(f(x) = \frac{1}{x}\) at \(x = 0\).

\(f(0)\) is undefined, and \(\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{1}{x}\) doesn't exist, so \(f\) has an infinite discontinuity at \(x = 0\).

5
Make Continuous
Find \(k\) so that \(f(x) = \begin{cases} 2x + k & x < 2 \\ x^2 & x \geq 2 \end{cases}\) is continuous.

Left limit: \(\lim_{x \to 2^-} (2x + k) = 4 + k\)

Right limit: \(\lim_{x \to 2^+} x^2 = 4\)

\(f(2) = 4\)

For continuity: \(4 + k = 4 \Rightarrow k = 0\)

6
Trigonometric
Is \(f(x) = \sin x\) continuous at \(x = \pi\)?

\(f(\pi) = \sin \pi = 0\)

\(\lim_{x \to \pi} \sin x = 0\)

Yes, continuous at \(x = \pi\).

7
Exponential
Is \(f(x) = e^x\) continuous at \(x = 0\)?

\(f(0) = 1\)

\(\lim_{x \to 0} e^x = 1\)

Yes, continuous at \(x = 0\).

1.8

The Definition of the Limit

Theory and Formulas

ε-δ Definition: \(\lim_{x \to a} f(x) = L\) if for every \(\epsilon > 0\), there exists \(\delta > 0\) such that:

\[ 0 < |x - a| < \delta \implies |f(x) - L| < \epsilon \]

Examples (7+)

1
Linear
Use ε-δ to prove \(\lim_{x \to 2} (3x - 1) = 5\).

Given \(\epsilon > 0\), choose \(\delta = \frac{\epsilon}{3}\).

If \(0 < |x-2| < \delta\), then \(|(3x-1)-5| = |3x-6| = 3|x-2| < 3\delta = \epsilon\).

2
Constant
Use ε-δ to prove \(\lim_{x \to a} c = c\).

Given any \(\epsilon > 0\), choose any \(\delta > 0\).

If \(0 < |x-a| < \delta\), then \(|c - c| = 0 < \epsilon\).

3
Quadratic
Use ε-δ to prove \(\lim_{x \to 3} x^2 = 9\).

Given \(\epsilon > 0\), choose \(\delta = \min(1, \frac{\epsilon}{7})\).

If \(0 < |x-3| < \delta\), then \(|x^2-9| = |x-3||x+3| < \delta(|x-3|+6) < \delta(1+6) = 7\delta \leq \epsilon\).

4
Finding δ
For \(\lim_{x \to 4} (2x+1) = 9\), find δ for ε = 0.1.

\(|(2x+1)-9| = 2|x-4| < \epsilon\)

So \(|x-4| < \frac{\epsilon}{2} = 0.05\)

Choose \(\delta = 0.05\).

5
Linear Function
Use ε-δ to prove \(\lim_{x \to 1} (5x-2) = 3\).

Given \(\epsilon > 0\), choose \(\delta = \frac{\epsilon}{5}\).

If \(0 < |x-1| < \delta\), then \(|(5x-2)-3| = 5|x-1| < 5\delta = \epsilon\).

6
Finding ε
For \(\lim_{x \to 2} (3x+1) = 7\), if δ = 0.01, find the corresponding ε.

\(|(3x+1)-7| = 3|x-2| < 3\delta = 0.03\)

So ε = 0.03.

7
Understanding
Explain why \(\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{1}{x}\) does not exist using ε-δ definition.

For any proposed limit L, choose ε = 1. No matter how small δ is, we can find x with \(0 < |x| < \delta\) but \(|\frac{1}{x} - L| \geq 1\).

2.1

The Definition of the Derivative

Theory and Formulas

Definition:

\[ f'(x) = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f(x+h) - f(x)}{h} \]

Alternative: \(f'(a) = \lim_{x \to a} \frac{f(x) - f(a)}{x - a}\)

Examples (7+)

1
Polynomial
Use definition to find \(f'(x)\) for \(f(x) = x^2 + 3x\).

\(f'(x) = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{(x+h)^2 + 3(x+h) - (x^2+3x)}{h}\)

\(= \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{x^2+2xh+h^2+3x+3h-x^2-3x}{h} = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{2xh+h^2+3h}{h}\)

\(= \lim_{h \to 0} (2x + h + 3) = 2x + 3\)

2
Square Root
Use definition to find \(f'(x)\) for \(f(x) = \sqrt{x}\).

\(f'(x) = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{\sqrt{x+h} - \sqrt{x}}{h} \cdot \frac{\sqrt{x+h}+\sqrt{x}}{\sqrt{x+h}+\sqrt{x}}\)

\(= \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{(x+h)-x}{h(\sqrt{x+h}+\sqrt{x})} = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{h}{h(\sqrt{x+h}+\sqrt{x})}\)

\(= \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{1}{\sqrt{x+h}+\sqrt{x}} = \frac{1}{2\sqrt{x}}\)

3
Rational
Use definition to find \(f'(x)\) for \(f(x) = \frac{1}{x}\).

\(f'(x) = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{\frac{1}{x+h} - \frac{1}{x}}{h} = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{x - (x+h)}{hx(x+h)}\)

\(= \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{-h}{hx(x+h)} = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{-1}{x(x+h)} = -\frac{1}{x^2}\)

4
Cubic
Use definition to find \(f'(x)\) for \(f(x) = x^3\).

\(f'(x) = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{(x+h)^3 - x^3}{h} = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{x^3+3x^2h+3xh^2+h^3-x^3}{h}\)

\(= \lim_{h \to 0} (3x^2 + 3xh + h^2) = 3x^2\)

5
Constant
Use definition to find \(f'(x)\) for \(f(x) = c\) (constant).

\(f'(x) = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{c - c}{h} = \lim_{h \to 0} 0 = 0\)

6
Linear
Use definition to find \(f'(x)\) for \(f(x) = mx + b\).

\(f'(x) = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{m(x+h)+b - (mx+b)}{h} = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{mh}{h} = m\)

7
At a Point
Use definition to find \(f'(2)\) for \(f(x) = x^2\).

\(f'(2) = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{(2+h)^2 - 4}{h} = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{4+4h+h^2-4}{h}\)

\(= \lim_{h \to 0} (4 + h) = 4\)

2.2

Interpretation of the Derivative

Theory and Formulas

Interpretations:

  1. Slope of tangent line
  2. Instantaneous rate of change
  3. Velocity (if position function)
  4. Acceleration (if velocity function)
  5. Marginal cost/revenue (in economics)

Examples (7+)

1
Tangent Line
Find equation of tangent to \(f(x) = x^3 - 3x + 1\) at \(x = 2\).

\(f'(x) = 3x^2 - 3\), \(f'(2) = 9\)

\(f(2) = 8 - 6 + 1 = 3\)

Equation: \(y - 3 = 9(x - 2)\) or \(y = 9x - 15\)

2
Physics
Position: \(s(t) = t^3 - 6t^2 + 9t\). Find velocity and acceleration at \(t=2\).

\(v(t) = s'(t) = 3t^2 - 12t + 9\), \(v(2) = -3\)

\(a(t) = v'(t) = 6t - 12\), \(a(2) = 0\)

3
Economics
Cost function: \(C(x) = 1000 + 5x + 0.01x^2\). Find marginal cost at \(x=100\).

\(C'(x) = 5 + 0.02x\), \(C'(100) = 5 + 2 = 7\)

Marginal cost = \$7 per additional unit

4
Tangent Line
Find where tangent to \(f(x) = x^2\) is parallel to \(y = 4x - 1\).

Slope of line = 4

\(f'(x) = 2x = 4 \Rightarrow x = 2\)

Point: \((2, 4)\)

5
Normal Line
Find equation of normal line to \(f(x) = \sqrt{x}\) at \(x=4\).

\(f'(x) = \frac{1}{2\sqrt{x}}\), \(f'(4) = \frac{1}{4}\)

Normal slope = \(-4\) (negative reciprocal)

\(f(4) = 2\), Equation: \(y - 2 = -4(x - 4)\)

6
Physics
Height: \(h(t) = -4.9t^2 + 20t + 10\). Find velocity when \(t=2\).

\(v(t) = h'(t) = -9.8t + 20\), \(v(2) = -19.6 + 20 = 0.4\) m/s

7
Rate of Change
Area of circle: \(A(r) = \pi r^2\). Find rate of change of area w.r.t. radius at \(r=3\).

\(A'(r) = 2\pi r\), \(A'(3) = 6\pi\)

When radius = 3, area increases at \(6\pi\) units² per unit increase in radius.
